I am...

... a student in my 4th year of Electrical Engineering program at University of British Columbia with an immense interest in programming.


Back during grade 11 in highschool, I had an option to have one class off or to plug that gap with a some random elective. Having nothing to spend my time on, I chose the latter. The "some random elective" happened to be introduction to programming.


Having a variety of languages to choose from, I closed my eyes and hit C++ (Quite happy I did now). The lessons were actually just huge PDF files. To a highschool student with no experience coding, this was quite odd. As much as I tried, it felt as if the teacher is just being lazy and not willing to teach, making me dislike the class. Skipping to present, I'm glad it happened the way it did. Without realizing, I learned one of, if not, the most important skill in programming - teaching yourself. Whether the instructor realized it or not, I'm thankful for having him.


Going back to my first year of university, I was interested in computer technology; however, at the time, I couldn't decide which area appealed to me the most as I simply haven't had enough experience to judge. Given that, I chose to do electrical engineering which provided me with a very wide range to choose from.


Skipping another few years, I finished my third year. Working through many projects in school and outside, I learned that higher level languages are far more appealing to me. By the end of the ELEC program my goal is to try as many software areas as possible and choose something I can do indefinitely.
